Should we slow down AI for safety’s sake?

Vitalik Buterin, the co-founder of Ethereum, has proposed an intriguing concept: a “soft pause” in global computing power to address the potential risks of super-intelligent AI. His suggestion involves reducing computing capacity by 90-99% for a period of 1-2 years, essentially buying humanity time to prepare for the implications of advanced AI systems.

This proposal raises fascinating questions about the balance between innovation and safety in AI development. While some advocate for accelerated progress (d/acc), others emphasize the importance of careful consideration and preparation.

Key Points from Vitalik’s Proposal

  • Temporary reduction in global computing power
  • 90-99% decrease for 1-2 years
  • Focus on preparation and safety measures
  • Aim to mitigate risks of super-intelligent AI

It’s a thought-provoking perspective from a significant figure in the tech world, regardless of one’s personal views on his past work or decisions. The proposal opens up important discussions about AI safety, innovation pacing, and global coordination.
